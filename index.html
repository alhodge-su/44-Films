What is 44 Films?

44 Films is a project started by The Sports Media Center at Syracuse University's S.I. Newhouse School of Public Communications, in conjunction with the Syracuse Department of Athletics focused on short form non-fiction narrative filmmaking that puts the students at the forefront of every aspect of production, with the guidance of faculty and professional staff.

Recent Releases

Otto's Origins

Dating back to 1978, Otto's Origins looks at the evolution of how the popular Syracuse mascot came to be. Originally a Native American chief known as "Saltine Warrior," the beloved orange went through many iterations over the years. This is the story of how the juiced-up, bumbling citrus fruit from which two legs protrude scootered into our hearts.

Playing for Kelly

Kelly Seubert spent 13 years as an administrative assistant for Jim Boeheim, working in the background for the good of Syracuse basketball. Her son, Chris LaValle, will wear No. 13 for the Syracuse men’s basketball program this season, a number that represents those 13 years. He’ll be a walk-on, working in the background for the good of Syracuse basketball. Like Seubert, like son.

The Return of Otto's Army

From leading chants at the football, to overnight campouts before basketball games, to road-trips to support our teams, joining Otto’s Army means getting involved with the loudest and proudest school spirit in the nation. This is the story of the Syracuse student section, coming back stronger than ever following over a year of uncertainty during COVID.

FLJ: Rise to the Rafters

Orange basketball legend, hall of famer and current head coach of the women’s basketball team at ‘Cuse, Felisha Legette-Jack, has always been evolving on and off the court. From her graduation from Syracuse in ‘89 to her return to her alma mater in 2022, she has achieved numerous successes. In November 2021, Legette-Jack became the first female student-athlete in Syracuse Athletics history to have her Jersey retired.
